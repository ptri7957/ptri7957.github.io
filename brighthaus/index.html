What is Passive House?

Passive House (or Passivhaus) building principles originated in Germany and are a housing performance standard designed to deliver houses that maintain a healthy air quality and a stable internal temperature, regardless of the external climate conditions. These building principles have now spread throughout Europe and to many other countries where people have recognised the benefits that come with building to an exact and measurable standard. The Passive House building standard is based on five key principles of construction.

The average new Australian home achieves 15.4 ACH50 (air changes per hour at 50 Pascals of pressure difference) on a blower door test. A passive house must score below a 0.6 ACH50 to pass certification.

Mechanical Ventilation
Read More

The extraordinary air tightness of a Passive House means that mechanical ventilation in the form of a Heat Recovery Ventilation (HRV) system is required to introduce fresh air into the house when all of the windows and doors are closed. The HRV system operates to extract the stale indoor air, replacing it with fresh filtered outdoor air. Mechanical ventilation can greatly assist in reducing indoor air pollution and mitigate health risks for allergy sufferers.

Thermal Bridge-free Construction
Read More

A thermal bridges is a path that heat or cold can pass along, which can bypass insulation and transfer temperature variants into a building. Eliminating as many thermal bridges in the building structure as possible will assist with ensuring that external heat or cold is not transferred inside the building, preventing the occurrence of hot spots or cold spots and helping to maintain a stable internal temperature throughout.

Thermal Insulation
Read More

High quality insulation is required in passive houses to provide proper thermal separation between the heated or cooled inside environment and the outdoors. A continuous layer of insulation in the floors, walls and ceilings improves thermal comfort, reduces the risk of condensation, mould and mildew and lowers energy costs associated with heating and cooling.
